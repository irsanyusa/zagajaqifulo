Junior Pope: Akwa Ibom State Governor Orders Body of Makeup Artist to Be Brought Back Home

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, June 24, 2024
  • The remains of the makeup artist involved in the tragic boat accident that killed Junior Pope and others have been ordered to be sent to Akwa Ibom state
  • Legit.ng reported that Abigail Fredrick's corpse was buried at the river bank after her father failed to gather enough money needed for some traditional rites
  • The Akwa Ibom state governor has intervened in the matter as he released a public statement that has impressed a lot of Nigerians

The Akwa Ibom state government, under the watch of Pastor Umo Bassey Eno, has demanded the body of their daughter, Abigail Fredrick, be returned for proper burial arrangements.

Abigail was a professional makeup artist who was tragically involved in the boat accident that took the life of famous Nigerian actor Pope Obumneme Odonwodo, aka Junior Pope, and some others.

The young creative’s body was discovered on Thursday, April 11, and was buried along the riverbank.

The townspeople allegedly ordered she be buried there since her family could not afford the payment for certain traditional sacrifices for her corpse to be taken back home to Akwa Ibom for burial.

The statement from the Akwa Ibom government read in part:

“We are deeply saddened by her painful passing. On behalf of the Government and people of AKwa Ibom State, we sympathize with her family, the Nollywood community, and her friends and colleagues over this sad loss.“We have also followed and read the reportage, especially on the social media platforms surrounding her burial and wish to state categorically that, government was not informed of the circumstances that led to her burial.“His Excellency, Governor Umo Eno, has consequently directed that her body be brought back to her family for a proper and decent burial. As the famous theme song of the Liverpool Football Club goes, the bereaved family will not “walk alone”.
