Best Baby Monitors for the Ultimate Peace Of Mind

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, August 5, 2024

Being a new parent (or even a seasoned parent!) can be nerve wracking. Of course, ensuring your baby’s safety and well-being becomes a top priority, which is why a vital thing to have before the little one arrives is a baby monitor. Luckily, in today’s digital age, there are so many on the market, each boasting unique features and functionalities. That said, navigating through this sea of options can be daunting, leaving parents perplexed about which monitor best suits their needs. I took a deep dive into the realm of baby monitors, exploring the factors that demand consideration when making this pivotal purchase. From range and coverage to security and ease of use, I unravel the essential elements that can make or break the monitoring experience for parents.

What to consider when buying a baby monitor

Just like when shopping for baby swings and baby carriers, there are factors to consider to ensure you choose the right baby monitor for your needs. Here are four key things to keep in mind.

Range and coverage

Consider the size of your home and the range you need the baby monitor to cover. If you have a larger house or plan to use the monitor outdoors, you'll need one with a longer range. A range of 1,000-1,200 feet can be expected for longer range baby monitors, though consider that factors like house layout, walls, and other features can impact the range. (So, even if you have a small home, lean towards purchasing a monitor with a higher range when possible.)

Features and functionality

Baby monitors come with a variety of features beyond just audio and video monitoring. Some may offer temperature sensors, night vision, two-way communication, lullabies, motion detection, or even integration with smartphone apps. Think about which features are important to you.

Security and privacy

With concerns about privacy and hacking, it's important to choose a baby monitor with robust security features. Look for monitors that offer encryption for secure transmission of audio and video signals. You’ll want to avoid monitors with known security vulnerabilities and opt for reputable brands that prioritize user privacy.

Ease of use and portability

Depending on how tech savvy you are, setting up and using a baby monitor can be complicated. Consider how easy the monitor is to set up and use and look for models with intuitive interfaces and clear instructions. Portability is important if you plan to travel with the monitor or move it between rooms frequently. Choose a monitor that’s lightweight and easy to carry around.

How to use and maintain a baby monitor

Here's a guide to help you get the most out of your monitor and effectively maintain it over time.

Set up

Place the camera unit in the nursery and position it to get a clear view of the baby's crib, per the manufacturer’s instructions. Ensure the monitor is securely mounted or placed to prevent it from falling.

Power on

Plug in the camera unit and the parent unit or smartphone app to power on the baby monitor. Make sure both units are fully charged or connected to power sources as needed.

Connectivity

Establish a secure connection between the camera unit and the parent unit or smartphone app. This may involve pairing the devices via Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or other wireless technologies. Follow the set-up instructions provided by the manufacturer to ensure a stable and reliable connection.

Adjust settings

Customize the settings of the baby monitor to suit your needs. This may include adjusting the volume level, selecting video quality settings, enabling alerts for motion or sound detection, and configuring other features offered by the monitor.

Monitor baby

Once the monitor is set up and connected, use it to monitor your baby's activities and health. Keep the parent unit or smartphone app nearby so you can easily check on your baby.

Maintain cleanliness

Regularly clean the camera lens and parent unit or smartphone app to ensure clear video and audio quality.

Check connections

Periodically check the connections and cables of the baby monitor to ensure they are secure and undamaged. Loose or damaged connections can result in unreliable performance or interruptions in the monitoring signal.

Update firmware

Make sure to install them according to the manufacturer's instructions. Updating the firmware can improve performance, add new features, and address security vulnerabilities.

Replace batteries

If the baby monitor uses rechargeable batteries, monitor their status regularly and recharge or replace them as needed to ensure uninterrupted operation.

Installation and setup guide

For installation and setup, here’s what you need to do:

Choose a location

Place the camera in a secure location with a clear view of your baby. The parent unit should be within reach and audible wherever you'll be using it.

Follow the manual's instructions

Each model has specific steps for pairing the units, connecting to Wi-Fi (if applicable), and activating features. The manual will guide you through the process, including for app setup.

Test

Once set up, test all features of the monitor to ensure audio, video, and any additional functionalities work properly. As for troubleshooting, you want to identify the issue, check the basics (i.e., ensure both units are powered on, plugged in, batteries are charged, etc.), consult the manual, and check online resources if need be.

Smart features and connectivity

Some of the smart features available in baby monitors include smartphone connectivity, app integration, video and audio monitoring, movement and sound detection, room temperature and humidity monitoring, sleep tracking, growth tracking, feeding and diaper change monitoring, and more.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Do pediatricians recommend baby monitors?

Pediatrician at Akron Children’s, Sarah Adams, MD, says home monitoring devices, especially wearable monitors, are not necessarily needed if your baby is healthy and a monitor has not been recommended by your pediatrician. “It is very important to follow safe sleeping guidelines recommended by the AAP and to discuss using a baby monitor with your pediatrician,” Dr. Adams says. “Use of a non-contact baby monitor can offer video and audio monitoring if your baby wakes up.”

Are baby monitors a good idea?

There is no evidence that commercial devices to monitor infants reduce the risk of SIDS, says Jennifer Leonardo, MSW, PhD, head of the Children’s Safety Network at Education Development Center (EDC). There is even some concern that parents may use such devices for a sense of security, but they should not be used as a replacement for adhering to other safe sleep guidelines. Parents should consult the American Academy of Pediatrics 2022 guidelines for reducing SIDS.

Dr. Adams says non-contact baby monitors offer some reassurance to families but do not replace the need to follow safe sleep guidelines. “The wearable monitors for an otherwise healthy baby may cause more harm than good and signal falsely,” she says.

Is it ok to use a baby monitor for a newborn?

Baby monitors can give parents peace of mind and are ok to use for a newborn, Dr. Adams notes. She recommends using a non-contact monitor about six feet away from the baby. “A wearable monitor should be used if recommended by your pediatrician,” she says. “It is recommended that newborns sleep alone, on their back and in cribs or bassinets in the parents room due to frequent waking and feeding.”

How do I choose between a Wi-Fi and non-Wi-Fi baby monitor?

Dr. Adams recommends a non-Wi-Fi baby monitor if you are concerned about connectivity and privacy safety.
