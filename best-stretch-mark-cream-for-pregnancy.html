What to consider when buying stretch mark creams for pregnancy

Risks and cautions

“During pregnancy, it's advisable for women to be cautious about the skincare products they use, as certain ingredients may pose potential risks to the developing fetus,” states Dr. Andrew Ordon, a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon in Beverly Hills, California. “Retinoids, including prescription forms like Retin-A and over-the-counter retinol, should be used sparingly due to concerns about high doses of vitamin A contributing to birth defects. Skincare products containing hydroquinone, a skin-lightening agent, and certain preservatives like formaldehyde and parabens should be approached with caution, and pregnant women may opt for products labeled as pregnancy-safe." Always consult your healthcare provider before trying a new product, and perform patch tests on your skin to see if you have any sensitivities.

Efficacy

Look at reviews that tell you how the cream works, but be cognizant of who the people are. You want reviews from people who have skin similar to yours.

Ingredients

Of course, avoid any known allergens that might affect you, and doctors say some should be mindful of retinoids during pregnancy.

Brand integrity

As many consider this the most sensitive time for any topical application, I chose brands with solid backgrounds that are easily purchased.

Application

Check how often you have to use the product and how to apply. Some products also require unique storage capabilities, like refrigeration.

Reviews

I looked for reviews by pregnant women in their first trimester, second trimester, and third trimester (and even beyond!), as well as feedback from obstetricians, gynecologists, and dermatologists.

Price point and product size

The value will vary based on product size and price.

Pregnant women should always consult their doctor before using stretch mark creams or other skin products. Some ingredients can have adverse effects on the fetus both before and after birth.

Do pregnancy stretch mark creams work?

There are many stretch mark creams, body butters, belly butters, and stretch mark serums that promise excellent results. However, there is no clinical evidence that stretch marks can be prevented entirely but, instead, are determined by genetics. Hydrating the skin certainly helps push against genetics, and there is no harm in keeping your skin supple and moist.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

When should you start using stretch mark cream in pregnancy?

In general, it’s recommended to start using stretch mark creams early in pregnancy, ideally in the first trimester (or even while you’re trying to get pregnant). Late is better than never on this front, but starting skin care early will help keep your skin moist and firm as your belly grows.

How does a stretch mark cream vary from other lotions?

You wouldn’t ask your gyno for the best eye creams, so don’t ask the lady from spin class who’s never had kids for stretch mark cream recs. Ensure that you’re choosing pregnancy-safe products that avoid certain ingredients that might harm you or your baby. Instead of lumping in stretch mark creams with the best anti-aging eye creams or cellulite-reducing lotions, I focused specifically on creams made for pregnant people and all three trimesters.

What is the difference between stretch mark oil, cream, butter, and balm?

Creams, balms, and oils have different ratios of water and oil. Many mothers choose a formulation based on how it works for their skin type and lifestyle. Stretch mark cream consists of equal (or near equal) amounts of oil and water. The water content promotes absorption, and the oil content traps moisturizing or healing ingredients on the skin's surface, protecting dry or damaged skin.

Balms contain fatty oils and wax instead of water to maintain a thick formula. While they might protect more, they usually take longer to absorb into the skin. Stretch mark oils or serums take more time to absorb than creams, butters and lotions, but once absorbed, they deeply moisturize and heal the skin. Some reviews mention oils staining clothing, but this also depends on the type of clothing and application ritual used. Stretch mark oils are usually noted for their higher levels of absorption.

How do I avoid stretch marks during pregnancy?

Stretch marks are hormonally and genetically determined and can’t really be controlled. Many people want to know how to prevent stretch marks during pregnancy, but unfortunately, they cannot be completely avoided. There are some things you can do to help prevent them from occurring or minimize their appearance, such as keeping your skin moisturized by using lotion or cream every day. You should also drink lots of water, which helps your body stay hydrated and will make your skin softer and less prone to stretch marks. Many doctors also recommend eating a balanced diet rich in zinc, protein, and vitamins A, C, and D to promote healthy skin.

Does moisturizing prevent stretch marks in pregnancy?

Stretch mark creams cannot completely prevent or eliminate the formation of stretch marks, whether they are in your genetic code or not, but they can help keep your skin optimally hydrated and supple to help prevent them. Applying a great moisturizer early and often can help keep your skin elastic and soft as your belly grows.

How often should I moisturize my pregnant belly?

It is most effective to moisturize early and often–at least once per day, but some mamas recommend it three to five times per day.
